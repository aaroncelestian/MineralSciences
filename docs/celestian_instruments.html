Current capabilities (operational)
Powder X-ray diffractometer (PXRD)
Operational
Phase identification, Rietveld refinement, and quantitative mineralogy. Workhorse instrument for LMO characterization, environmental mineral identification, holotype benchmark work (NH4Br), and XRDmatch software validation.
LMO work XRDmatch Phase ID Rietveld
X-ray fluorescence (XRF) microscope
Operational
Elemental mapping and bulk chemistry of minerals, soils, and environmental samples. Supports Pb entombment studies, REE recovery work, and CST characterization. Non-destructive analysis enables collections-based research.
Pb sequestration REE recovery Non-destructive
LIBS (laser-induced breakdown spectroscopy)
Operational
Rapid elemental analysis with minimal sample preparation. Supports field-analogous measurements, critical element screening in brine samples, and community toxin monitoring platform development. Unique capability for in-situ screening workflows.
Critical elements Brine screening Community science
Micro-CT scanner
NHM facility
3D internal structure visualization without destructive sectioning. Critical for kidney stone 3D morphology, internal porosity of MOF mineral drug carriers, and internal structure of fossil specimens including coprolite feather preservation work.
Kidney stones Drug delivery Paleontology
SEM-EDS
NHM facility
Scanning electron microscopy with energy-dispersive spectroscopy for high-resolution imaging and elemental analysis. Supports Magnéli phase characterization, biofilm-mineral interface imaging in kidney stone work, and new mineral morphology documentation.
Magnéli phases Biofilm interfaces New minerals
